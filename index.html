How to Use the Zwift Wheel Optimizer

Getting Started

Understanding the Basics

The Zwift Wheel Optimizer helps you choose the best wheels and frames for your virtual rides based on:

  • Your rider characteristics (weight, height, FTP)
  • The course profile (distance, elevation)
  • Your estimated power output

Key Concepts

Time Savings: The estimated seconds you'll save over the course distance with a particular wheel/frame combination.

Flat vs Climb Performance: Some wheels perform better on flat sections, others excel on climbs.

Frame Levels: As you level up frames in Zwift, their performance improves.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Enter Rider Details

Start by entering your weight, height, and FTP. These factors affect how equipment performs for you.

Tip: Be as accurate as possible for best results.

2. Select Your Course

Choose a Zwift route or create a custom one by specifying distance and elevation.

Tip: The optimizer adjusts recommendations based on course profile.

3. Set Power Output

Indicate your expected power output, either as absolute watts or percentage of FTP.

Tip: Higher power favors aero equipment, lower power favors lightweight.

4. Choose Bike Types

Select which bike types to consider (Road, TT, MTB, etc.).

Tip: Narrow down to specific types for more focused recommendations.

5. Apply Filters

Filter wheels and frames by brand if you have preferences.

Tip: Use the search function to find specific models.

6. Calculate & Compare

Click "Calculate" to see recommendations and compare options.

Tip: Click on charts or tables to highlight specific equipment.

Understanding the Results

Performance Charts

The charts visualize how different wheels and frames perform on your selected course:

  • Blue bars show flat section performance
  • Red bars show climbing performance
  • Taller bars indicate better time savings

Recommendation Tables

The tables rank equipment by total time savings:

  • Wheel recommendations show estimated seconds saved
  • Frame recommendations show current performance and potential gains from leveling up

Best Equipment Summary

This combines the best frame and wheel recommendations to show the optimal setup for your ride.

Advanced Features

Custom Wheels

Add your own custom wheel specifications to compare against Zwift's inventory.

How to: Use the "Add Custom Wheels" section at the bottom of the Optimizer tab.

Frame Levels

Track your frame progression and see how leveling up affects performance.

How to: Use the Frame Levels tab to set your current frame levels.

Fueling Plan

Get nutrition and hydration recommendations based on ride duration and intensity.

How to: This is automatically calculated when you enter ride details.

Training Recommendations

Get course-specific training advice to prepare for your target route.

How to: These appear after calculating with a selected route.

About Zwift Wheel Optimizer Pro

What is this tool?

The Zwift Wheel Optimizer Pro is a comprehensive tool designed to help Zwift cyclists maximize their virtual performance by selecting the optimal equipment combinations for any given course.

It analyzes:

  • Rider characteristics (weight, height, power output)
  • Course profile (distance, elevation, terrain)
  • Equipment specifications (wheels, frames, levels)

To provide personalized recommendations that can save you valuable seconds on your rides.

How are calculations made?

The optimizer uses a sophisticated algorithm that considers:

  • Aerodynamics: More important on flat courses and at higher speeds
  • Weight: More important on hilly courses and for lighter riders
  • Power Output: Affects the relative importance of aero vs weight
  • Course Profile: Determines the balance between flat and climbing performance

All calculations are based on publicly available data about Zwift's equipment performance characteristics.

Data Sources

The tool uses equipment data from:

  • Zwift Insider's equipment tests
  • Community-collected performance data
  • User-submitted custom wheel specifications

All data is stored locally in your browser for privacy and performance.

Future Development

Planned features include:

  • More detailed course analysis
  • Rider position optimization
  • Drafting effect calculations
  • Zwift Power integration
  • Mobile app version
